## Requirements for Foreigners: EU vs Non-EU

The first fundamental distinction concerns your nationality. If you are a citizen of an EU country, the process is much simpler. You can start your business under the same conditions as an Italian citizen, without particular bureaucratic constraints.

For the first three months, you will only need a valid identity card. Subsequently, you will need to register with the registry office of the municipality where you intend to reside and make a declaration of presence at a police station. You will not need any specific residence permit for self-employment.

The situation changes significantly if you come from a non-EU country. In this case, you will need to obtain a residence permit for self-employment, which is essential to carry out any entrepreneurial activity.
