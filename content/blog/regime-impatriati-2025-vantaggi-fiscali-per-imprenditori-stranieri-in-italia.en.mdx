## The step-by-step process to obtain the regime

Moving to Italy and obtaining the impatriate regime is not complicated, but it requires **planning and precision**.

### Phase 1: Preliminary assessment (3-6 months before)

First of all, check if you meet the requirements:

- Ability to demonstrate value for the Italian economy
- Capacity to maintain residency in Italy for at least 4 years

**Useful tools**: Consultation with a specialized accountant, tax simulation, cost-benefit analysis.

### Phase 2: Document preparation (2-3 months before)

Gather all necessary documentation:
- Certificates of professional competence
- Work references
- Business plan in Italy
- Financial documentation

**Practical advice**: Translate all documents into Italian and have them apostilled in your country of origin.

### Phase 3: Actual move (month 0)

The move must be real and demonstrable:
- Registration with the Italian municipality
- Opening an Italian bank account
- Lease or purchase contract for a property
- Activation of utilities

**Attention**: The date of transfer of tax residence determines the start of the regime.

### Phase 4: Request for the regime (within 3 months)

Submit the request to the Revenue Agency with:
- Completed specific form
- Professional documentation
- Proof of residence transfer
- Business plan in Italy

**Timing**: The Revenue Agency has 90 days to respond.

### Phase 5: Annual management

Once the regime is obtained:
- Annual income tax declaration
- Maintenance of requirements
- Documentation of activities carried out
- Monitoring of deadlines
