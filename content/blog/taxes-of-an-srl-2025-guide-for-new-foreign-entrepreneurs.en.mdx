A **Limited Liability Company (SRL)** is one of the most commonly used forms of business in Italy. It has many advantages but also has **tax obligations**. Knowing them is essential for the success of your company.

## What is an SRL

An SRL is a capital company. This means that your personal assets are separate from those of the company. If the company has debts, your personal belongings are safe. This is a significant advantage.

To open an SRL, a minimum capital is required. Since 2022, only 1 euro is needed. However, it is better to have at least 10,000 euros. This gives you more credibility with banks and suppliers.

The SRL pays taxes on the income it generates. It doesn't matter if you take the money or leave it in the company. Taxes are paid regardless. This is different from other types of companies.

## IRES: The Tax on Profits

IRES is the most important tax for an SRL. It is paid on the taxable income of the company. Taxable income is the profit after deducting all costs.

The standard IRES rate is 24%. This applies for all of 2025. It is the same for all SRLs in Italy. It doesn't matter where your headquarters are located.

**News for 2025: Reduced IRES to 20%**
Companies that meet certain conditions can pay only 20% instead of 24%. The conditions are:
- Allocate at least 80% of the 2024 profits to a special reserve
- Invest at least 30% of these profits in "Industry 4.0" or "5.0" instrumental goods
- Not reduce the number of employees compared to the average of the previous three years
- Hire new permanent workers
- Not have used redundancy funds (except in exceptional cases)

To calculate IRES you need to:
1. Add up all revenues for the year
2. Subtract all deductible costs  
3. Multiply the result by 24% (or 20% if you qualify for reduced IRES)

Deductible costs include: salaries, rents, materials, consulting fees, and depreciation. Not all costs can be deducted. Some have limits.

The IRES declaration is made every year. The deadline is November 30 of the following year. For example, for income in 2025, the declaration must be submitted by November 30, 2026.

IRES is paid in two installments. The first by June 30. The second by November 30. It can also be paid in a single payment by July 30.

## IRAP: The Regional Tax

IRAP is the Regional Tax on Productive Activities. It is a tax that varies from region to region. Each region decides its percentage.

IRAP is calculated on the net production value. This includes:
- Revenues from sales
- Inventory increases
- Work done for the company

Some costs can be deducted from the production value. But not all. For example, salaries and employee contributions are not fully deductible.

The base rate of IRAP in 2025 is 3.9%. Regions can increase it by up to 0.92 points. The main percentages are:
- Lombardy: 3.9%
- Lazio: 4.65%
- Campania: 4.97%
- Sicily: 4.97%
- Other regions: between 3.9% and 4.97%

Some activities pay less IRAP. For example, innovative start-ups receive discounts. Those who hire young people may also have reductions.

The IRAP declaration is made together with the IRES declaration. The payment deadlines are the same.

## VAT: The Consumption Tax

VAT is the Value Added Tax. It is not a tax you pay. Your customers pay it. You collect it and pay it to the state.

The VAT rates in Italy are:
- 4% for essential goods (bread, milk, medicines, books)
- 5% for some social and health services
- 10% for some services (transport, hotels, restaurants, domestic energy)
- 22% for everything else

When you sell a product or service, you add VAT to the price. The customer pays you the total. Every month or quarter, you pay the collected VAT to the Revenue Agency.

You can also deduct the VAT you pay for the company. For example, if you buy a computer for the office, you can deduct the VAT paid. This reduces the VAT to be paid.

To manage VAT you need to:
- Issue invoices with VAT
- Keep a record of sales
- Keep a record of purchases
- Make periodic settlements
- Submit the annual declaration

VAT deadlines are monthly or quarterly. It depends on the company's turnover. If you invoice less than 400,000 euros a year, you can choose the quarterly option.

## Social Security Contributions

The managing partners of an SRL must pay INPS contributions. Even if they do not take a salary. This has been a legal obligation since 2000.

**Two types of possible contributions:**

**Separate INPS Management** - For director compensation:
- Rate of 26.07% for professionals without other pension funds
- Rate of 24% if registered with other pension schemes
- Paid on compensation received as a director

**Merchant Management** - If you actively work in the company:
- Minimum fixed contribution: 4,549.70 euros for 2025
- Rate of 24.48% on income above 18,555 euros
- Paid in 4 installments: May, August, November, February

**Double Contribution**
If you are both a director AND actively work in the company, you must pay both contributions. This is the most expensive situation but also the most common.

Contributions are paid in four installments throughout the year. The deadlines are:
- May 16 (40% of the due amount)
- August 20 (20% of the due amount)
- November 16 (20% of the due amount)
- February 16 of the following year (20% of the due amount)

If you have employees, you must also pay their contributions. The percentage is different. It is about 33% of the gross salary.

## Other Taxes and Levies

In addition to the main taxes, an SRL also pays:

**Registration Tax** - Paid to register certain contracts. For example, rental contracts.

**Stamp Duty** - Paid on invoices over 77.47 euros. The amount is 2 euros per invoice.

**Municipal Taxes** - Each municipality has its own taxes. The most common is TARI (waste tax).

**RAI Fee** - If you have a TV in the office, you must pay the fee. It is 90 euros per year.

**Insurance Tax** - If you have insurance policies, you pay an additional tax.

These taxes are small. But it is important to know them. This way you avoid penalties and issues.

- Reduced or zero IRAP for the first years
- Contribution exemptions for employees

**New Businesses in the South** - If you open in the Mezzogiorno, you can have:

- Non-repayable grants

## Practical Tips for Foreigners

If you come from abroad, here are some useful tips:

**Tax Code** - This is the first document you need. Request it from the Revenue Agency. It is free and you get it immediately.

**VAT Number** - This is necessary to open the SRL. Always request it from the Revenue Agency. You can do it online or in person.

**Bank Account** - You need an Italian bank account for the SRL. Many banks open accounts for foreigners as well. Bring translated documents.

**Accountant** - It is very useful to have one. They help you with taxes and compliance. Choose someone who speaks your language.

**Accounting Software** - There are simple programs to keep track of finances. Many are available in multiple languages. They save you time and money.

**Training** - Attend courses on Italian taxation. Many are free. They help you understand how the system works better.

The most important thing is not to be afraid. The Italian tax system may seem complicated. But with the right help, it is manageable. Many foreigners have successfully opened SRLs in Italy.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ) - SRL Taxes 2025

### How much does it cost to open an SRL in Italy?
To open an SRL, a minimum capital of 1 euro is required. However, it is better to have at least 10,000 euros for greater credibility. The establishment costs are around 1,000-2,000 euros for notary fees and formalities.

### What is the highest tax for an SRL?
IRES is usually the highest tax. It is paid at 24% on profits (or 20% with reduced IRES). On a profit of 50,000 euros, you pay about 12,000 euros in IRES.

### Do managing partners always have to pay INPS contributions?
Yes, it has been mandatory since 2000. Even if they do not take a salary. The minimum contribution for 2025 is 4,549.70 euros for Merchant Management.

### Can I pay taxes in installments?
Yes. IRES and IRAP are paid in two installments: 40% by July and 60% by November. INPS contributions can also be paid in 4 installments throughout the year.

### Are there incentives for innovative start-ups?
Yes. Innovative start-ups can have: reduced or zero IRAP, contribution exemptions for employees, and tax credits for research and development.

### Do I have to pay VAT if I am in the flat-rate scheme?
No. The flat-rate scheme for individuals does not include VAT. However, **SRLs cannot access the flat-rate scheme**. All SRLs must manage VAT normally.

### Can I transfer the headquarters of the SRL to another region?
Yes, you can transfer the headquarters. But be careful: IRAP changes based on the region. Some regions have lower rates (like Lombardy at 3.9%).
